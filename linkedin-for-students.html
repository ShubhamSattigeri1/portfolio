LinkedIn improvements for students

Many student LinkedIn profiles are too vague. They mention being passionate and motivated, but they do not show what the person is actually learning, building, or aiming for. A better profile is specific, readable, and easy to trust.

Three sections that matter most

  • Headline: say what you are studying or building, not just that you are a student.
  • About section: write a short paragraph about interests, strengths, and the type of role you want.
  • Featured or projects: add links that show real work, not only certificates.

What recruiters often look for

Recruiters often want quick clarity: what role fits you, what stack you use, and whether your projects support your profile. A short but specific profile usually works better than a long generic one.

  • Clear target role or area of interest
  • Evidence of projects or internships
  • Links to GitHub, portfolio, or published work
